astromyth 
about generator gallery documentation

Astromyth is a generative poster system inspired by astronomy (not astrology!) and ancient civilizations. The intention behind this project is to bridge the macro-level futuristic nature of space, with the micro-level nuance of the way we identify these celestial bodies through ancient human narratives.

Drawing from myths, white text gently overlays perlin noise that has been put to a grid, creating mosaics out of images of ancient figures and structures. Planets, moons, and stars gently swirl over the posters, lending a softer and more buoyant contrast to the structure of the grid.

Future iterations will include titles and more customized body text layers. Quotes from other mythic sources such as the Ancient Egyptian Book of the Dead will be incorporated as mostly-legible decorative elements. Another potentiality is to use astronomical data collected by NASA to create data points for the code to generate images and/or patterns from. I will also explore the possibility of generating the images of figures and structures, rather than collecting images to run through an array.
